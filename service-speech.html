Speech sounds

When a child's speech is hard to understand, it affects confidence, friendships, and school. We work on the sounds they need — without making therapy feel like drills.

What it looks like

Therapy that fits into your child's world, not the other way around.

Speech sound difficulties come in different shapes: a child who can't make a particular sound (articulation), a child who follows patterns that simplify speech (phonology), or a child whose mouth and brain aren't yet coordinating sounds into words (motor planning). Each gets a different approach — matched to how your child learns best.

When to reach out
  • Family or friends often ask "what did she say?"
  • Specific sounds (r, l, s, th, k, g) are distorted past the typical age
  • Multiple sounds are missing or swapped
  • Speech sounds choppy or effortful
  • Child is becoming frustrated or avoiding talking

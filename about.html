Meet your SLP

Relationship-first, research-informed, deeply human.

I started Social House because I believe the best therapy happens in the places kids actually live — at the kitchen table, on the floor with their favorite toys, or in a quiet corner of the preschool classroom. Clinics can feel sterile. Connection can't be forced. So we don't.

My approach is neurodiversity-affirming, child-led, and family-partnered. That means we follow your child's lead, honor how their brain is wired, and treat caregivers as the most important people in the process — because they are.

Outside of sessions, I'm likely hiking a desert trail, re-reading a good novel, or planning a weekend with my own family. I know what it feels like to want the best for a child you love. That's the only lens I bring to work.
